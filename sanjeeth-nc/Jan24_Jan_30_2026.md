## Overview
This week focused on understanding and applying **time series forecasting techniques** to predict customer booking patterns for BimRide's ride-hailing platform in Barbados.  
The primary goal was to study foundational statistical models such as ARIMA and SARIMA, explore advanced machine learning approaches like Facebook Prophet and LSTM neural networks, and design a practical forecasting pipeline that integrates with BimRide's operational systems.

Accurate booking prediction enables BimRide to **optimize driver allocation, reduce passenger wait times, and maximize fleet utilization** across peak tourist seasons and local demand cycles. Understanding how historical ride data, weather patterns, flight schedules, and seasonal tourism trends interact is essential for building reliable forecasting models that support real-time business decisions.

## Dummy Project Work (Step by Step)

### Part 1: Time Series Forecasting Fundamentals

#### Step 1: Time Series Data Structure & Preprocessing
- Studied the characteristics of time series data: **trend, seasonality, cyclicality, and noise** components.  
- Analyzed stationarity requirements and how the **Augmented Dickey-Fuller (ADF) test** determines whether differencing is needed.  
- Explored data preprocessing techniques including **missing value imputation**, outlier detection, and resampling for uniform time intervals.  
- Investigated how BimRide's booking data exhibits weekly seasonality (weekend spikes) and annual seasonality (tourist high season from December–April).  
- Examined the impact of irregular events (hurricanes, Crop Over Festival, cruise ship arrivals) on booking volume and how to handle them as exogenous variables.

#### Step 2: Statistical Forecasting Models
Comprehensive study of classical time series methods:

| **Model** | **Core Mechanism** | **Strengths** | **BimRide Application** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ARIMA** | Autoregressive + Differencing + Moving Average | Captures linear trends and short-term patterns | Baseline daily booking prediction |
| **SARIMA** | ARIMA with seasonal component | Handles repeating seasonal patterns | Weekly and monthly demand cycles |
| **Exponential Smoothing (ETS)** | Weighted average of past observations | Simple, fast, interpretable | Quick short-term booking estimates |
| **Holt-Winters** | Triple exponential smoothing | Captures trend + seasonality simultaneously | Tourist season ramp-up forecasting |
| **STL Decomposition** | Seasonal-Trend decomposition using LOESS | Separates trend, seasonal, and residual | Isolating true demand growth from seasonal noise |

#### Step 3: Model Selection & Evaluation Criteria
- Studied **ACF (Autocorrelation Function)** and **PACF (Partial Autocorrelation Function)** plots for identifying optimal ARIMA parameters (p, d, q).  
- Analyzed the role of **AIC (Akaike Information Criterion)** and **BIC (Bayesian Information Criterion)** in comparing model fit while penalizing complexity.  
- Implemented **walk-forward validation** (expanding window cross-validation) as the gold standard for time series model evaluation.  
- Compared error metrics: **MAE, RMSE, MAPE, and sMAPE** for measuring forecast accuracy across different booking volume scales.

### Part 2: Advanced Forecasting Models for Booking Prediction

#### Step 4: Facebook Prophet for Demand Forecasting
- Studied Prophet's **additive decomposition model**: y(t) = g(t) + s(t) + h(t) + ε(t), where g = trend, s = seasonality, h = holidays, ε = error.  
- Analyzed Prophet's ability to handle **multiple seasonalities** (daily, weekly, annual) — critical for BimRide's overlapping demand cycles.  
- Explored the **holiday effects module** for modeling Barbados-specific events: Crop Over Festival, Independence Day, Easter weekend, and cruise ship schedules.  
- Tested Prophet's **changepoint detection** for identifying shifts in booking trends (e.g., post-marketing campaign surges or competitor entry).  
- Evaluated Prophet's advantage of requiring **minimal hyperparameter tuning** compared to traditional ARIMA, making it practical for rapid deployment.

#### Step 5: LSTM Neural Networks for Sequential Prediction
| **Component** | **Function** | **Advantage** | **BimRide Application** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Input Gate** | Controls new information flow | Selectively learns relevant patterns | Filter noise from booking spikes |
| **Forget Gate** | Discards irrelevant past data | Prevents outdated pattern influence | Adapt to post-pandemic travel shifts |
| **Output Gate** | Determines prediction output | Context-aware forecasting | Generate hourly booking predictions |
| **Cell State** | Long-term memory pathway | Captures distant dependencies | Learn annual tourism seasonality |
| **Sequence Windowing** | Sliding window input structure | Converts time series to supervised learning | Use past 30 days to predict next 7 days |

- Investigated **sequence-to-sequence (Seq2Seq)** architecture for multi-step booking forecasts (predicting next 7 or 14 days simultaneously).  
- Studied **bidirectional LSTM** variants for capturing both forward and backward temporal dependencies in booking patterns.  
- Analyzed **attention mechanisms** that allow the model to focus on the most relevant historical time steps when making predictions.  
- Explored data scaling requirements (**MinMaxScaler**) and the importance of proper train-test splitting that respects temporal ordering.

#### Step 6: Ensemble Methods & Model Stacking
- Designed a **weighted ensemble approach** combining Prophet (captures seasonality + holidays), SARIMA (captures short-term autocorrelation), and LSTM (captures non-linear patterns).  
- Studied **model stacking** where a meta-learner (e.g., Ridge Regression) combines base model predictions to minimize overall forecast error.  
- Analyzed how ensemble forecasts consistently **outperform individual models by 10–25%** on booking prediction benchmarks.  
- Explored **confidence interval generation** from ensemble predictions for risk-aware operational planning.

### Part 3: Forecasting Pipeline Design & Feature Engineering

#### Step 7: Exogenous Feature Integration
- Identified key **external variables** that influence BimRide bookings beyond historical ride data:
  - **Flight arrival data**: Grantley Adams International Airport inbound flight schedules  
  - **Weather forecasts**: Rainfall probability, temperature, and hurricane warnings  
  - **Hotel occupancy rates**: Accommodation booking data from partner properties  
  - **Cruise ship schedules**: Port of Bridgetown arrival and departure times  
  - **Local events calendar**: Festivals, concerts, sporting events, and public holidays  
- Studied **lag feature engineering** — using yesterday's weather, last week's bookings, and same-day-last-year comparisons as model inputs.  
- Explored **Fourier terms** as an alternative to dummy variables for encoding complex seasonal patterns.

#### Step 8: End-to-End Pipeline Architecture
| **Pipeline Stage** | **Tool / Method** | **Output** | **Refresh Frequency** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Data Ingestion** | Snowpipe + API connectors | Raw booking, weather, flight data | Real-time / Hourly |
| **Feature Engineering** | dbt transformations | Cleaned features with lag variables | Daily |
| **Model Training** | Prophet + LSTM + SARIMA | Trained model artifacts | Weekly retraining |
| **Prediction Generation** | Ensemble scoring pipeline | 7-day and 30-day booking forecasts | Daily |
| **Monitoring & Drift Detection** | Statistical drift tests | Model accuracy alerts | Continuous |
| **Visualization** | Dashboard integration | Forecast vs. actuals comparison | Real-time |

- Designed a **model retraining schedule** where models are automatically retrained weekly using the latest booking data to capture evolving patterns.  
- Implemented **prediction drift monitoring** using RMSE tracking and statistical tests to detect when model accuracy degrades below acceptable thresholds.  
- Planned integration with **Apache Airflow** for orchestrating the end-to-end pipeline from data ingestion through prediction delivery.

## Challenges Faced
1. **Stationarity Transformation** – Determining the correct differencing order for BimRide booking data, which exhibits both trend and multiple seasonal components simultaneously.  
2. **Seasonality Overlap** – Handling the interaction between weekly patterns (weekend vs. weekday demand) and annual tourism seasonality (high season December–April vs. low season) within a single model.  
3. **Exogenous Data Availability** – Sourcing reliable, real-time flight arrival and cruise ship schedule data for integration into forecasting models as external features.  
4. **LSTM Hyperparameter Tuning** – Balancing network depth, sequence length, and learning rate to avoid overfitting on limited historical booking data while maintaining predictive accuracy.  
5. **Ensemble Weight Optimization** – Determining optimal combination weights for Prophet, SARIMA, and LSTM outputs that adapt to different forecasting horizons (short-term vs. long-term).  
6. **Cold Start Problem** – Forecasting demand for newly launched service zones in Barbados where limited historical booking data is available for model training.

## BimRide Application (Ride-Hailing)

### Proactive Driver Allocation
Time series forecasting enables BimRide to **predict booking surges hours or days in advance**, allowing proactive driver scheduling and positioning. By forecasting demand spikes around flight arrivals at Grantley Adams Airport or cruise ship dockings at the Port of Bridgetown, the platform can pre-position drivers in high-demand zones, reducing passenger wait times from 15+ minutes to under 5 minutes while maximizing driver earnings through reduced idle time.

### Dynamic Pricing Intelligence
Accurate demand forecasts feed directly into BimRide's **dynamic pricing engine**. When models predict high-demand periods (Friday evenings, holiday weekends, Crop Over Festival), pricing can be adjusted proactively rather than reactively. This prevents both under-pricing during peak demand (lost revenue) and over-pricing during predicted low demand (lost customers), optimizing revenue per ride while maintaining competitive fairness.

### Fleet & Resource Planning
Long-range booking forecasts (30-day and seasonal) support **strategic fleet management decisions**. Predicting the December–April tourist season ramp-up enables BimRide to onboard additional drivers, schedule vehicle maintenance during predicted low-demand windows, and negotiate fuel contracts based on anticipated ride volumes. This transforms fleet management from reactive to data-driven, reducing operational costs by an estimated 15–20%.

### Marketing Campaign Optimization
Forecasting models identify **predicted low-demand periods** where targeted marketing campaigns and promotional pricing can stimulate bookings. By understanding when organic demand naturally dips (e.g., mid-week during off-season), BimRide can time promotional offers, loyalty rewards, and partnership campaigns to fill demand valleys rather than wasting marketing spend during periods where demand is already high.
