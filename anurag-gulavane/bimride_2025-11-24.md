
# Daily Progress Report – November 24, 2025

## Overview
Today I focused on **Bimride’s Multi-Layer Trust & Identity Verification System** — the core stack that ensures every rider and driver using the platform is a *real person*, safely verified, and operating within the boundaries of the law. Mobility platforms operate on trust: riders trust the driver who arrives to pick them up; drivers trust the rider entering their vehicle; and the company must trust that all interactions follow safety, legal, and operational guidelines.

A weak identity system leads to:
- impersonation  
- fraudulent accounts  
- stolen phones used to create bogus rider profiles  
- underage riders misusing the platform  
- suspended drivers creating new accounts  
- payment fraud  
- driver/rider mismatches  
- catastrophic safety incidents  

A strong identity system creates:
- confidence  
- safety  
- accountability  
- legal compliance  
- smooth insurance workflows  
- healthier platform dynamics  

Barbados has specific requirements:
- national ID verification  
- driver licensing categories  
- vehicle registration rules  
- insurance requirements  
- cultural sensitivity regarding privacy  
- tourism-related verification challenges (foreign IDs/passports)  

Today I designed the full identity trust stack — from KYC onboarding to real-time identity checks to periodic re-verification and fraud detection.

---

## Activities

### 1. Designed the multi-stage identity verification flow
Both riders and drivers follow a staged verification process:

1. **Device Verification**  
2. **Phone Number Verification**  
3. **Government ID Verification**  
4. **Selfie Match (Face Verification)**  
5. **Behavioral Identity Confirmation**  
6. **Periodic Re-Verification**

This layered approach makes impersonation significantly harder.

---

### 2. Built Device Verification Layer
Identity begins with the device itself.

The system fingerprints:
- hardware identifiers  
- OS version  
- SIM information  
- network characteristics  
- device integrity flags  
- jailbreak/root detection  

This creates a **Device Trust Score**.

If device risk is high:
- certain features restricted  
- additional verifications triggered  
- manual review required  

This prevents “throwaway devices” from being used for fraud.

---

### 3. Implemented secure phone number verification
Phone number verification uses:
- SMS OTP  
- VoIP risk detection  
- number recycling detection  
- region-based phone risk scoring  

Tourists often use roaming or foreign numbers; system adapts verification flows accordingly.

---

### 4. Built government ID verification for drivers
Drivers must submit:
- Barbados national ID or passport  
- Driver’s license (relevant category)  
- Vehicle registration  
- Insurance documents  
- Vehicle inspection document  

System checks:
- expiration dates  
- document integrity  
- hologram patterns  
- MRZ integrity for passports  
- name cross-checks across documents  
- face-photo match with selfie  

This ensures safe, legal operation.

---

### 5. Designed rider identity verification (lighter, but still rigorous)
Riders are not required to submit as many documents but still undergo:
- government ID verification  
- face verification  
- device + phone number trust scoring  

Tourists can use:
- passports  
- international driver’s licenses  
- other travel documents  

This improves safety for both sides.

---

### 6. Implemented selfie-based face verification
Drivers and riders take a selfie that:
- checks liveness  
- matches government ID photo  
- prevents impersonation  
- prevents account-sharing  

Face verification is required:
- at onboarding  
- at periodic intervals (randomized)  
- during suspicious behavior flags  
- after long periods of inactivity  

Liveness detection prevents photo/spoof attacks.

---

### 7. Built behavioral identity modeling
Beyond static documents, identity is reinforced by behavior.

Metrics include:
- device movement patterns  
- app usage habits  
- geolocation consistency  
- payment behavior  
- login times  
- driving patterns (for drivers)  

If behavior deviates significantly:
- identity re-check triggered  
- account temporarily restricted  

This catches account takeovers early.

---

### 8. Designed IP + location intelligence
System tracks:
- suspicious IP clusters  
- VPN/proxy usage patterns  
- unusual location jumps  
- multi-account device-sharing behavior  

If a driver logs in from two distant locations within minutes → red flag.  
If a rider account repeatedly switches countries in unnatural ways → re-verification.

---

### 9. Created the Trust Score System
Every rider and driver gets an evolving **Trust Score** based on:
- document verification  
- device trust  
- payment reliability  
- behavioral consistency  
- rating history  
- fraud flags  
- safety reports  

Low score → limited features, more checks.  
High score → faster matching, fewer interruptions.

---

### 10. Built periodic re-verification workflows
Compliance requires continuous trust.

Re-verification happens:
- every 90 days (drivers)  
- every 180 days (riders)  
- immediately for risky accounts  
- after major app updates  
- after device change  

Drivers must re-confirm:
- license  
- registration  
- insurance  

Prevents expired-document operation.

---

### 11. Integrated identity into safety engine
If SOS is triggered:
- identity data locked  
- incident workspace created  
- identity cross-referenced across past incidents  
- risk indicators elevated  

Identity → safety → operational response.

---

### 12. Built identity fraud detection engine
Detects:
- multiple accounts per person  
- shared devices between banned accounts  
- fake IDs  
- deepfake attempts  
- mismatched selfie/ID combos  
- device farms (cheap low-end phones used for scams)  

AI models flag:
- mismatched facial symmetry  
- lighting inconsistencies  
- background artifacts  

Manual review finalizes high-risk cases.

---

### 13. Created privacy-sensitive identity handling
Identity data is:
- encrypted at rest  
- encrypted in transit  
- stored in isolated vaults  
- accessible only via privilege-scoped services  

Tourists especially value privacy; system respects this fully.

---

### 14. Designed identity flows for poor connectivity
If verification fails due to bad network:
- fall back to compressed upload mode  
- retry scheduling  
- offline-capture caching  
- asynchronous verification  

Critical for rural and coastal zones.
