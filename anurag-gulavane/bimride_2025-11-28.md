
# Daily Progress Report – November 28, 2025

## Overview
Today I focused on **Bimride’s Customer Support Automation Platform & Resolution Engine** — the system that handles rider and driver issues, classifies them intelligently, prioritizes correctly, and resolves them with speed and fairness. Customer support is not a “side feature” in mobility; it is a core operational pillar that shapes public perception, prevents churn, protects driver morale, and closes safety loops.

A mobility business faces massive daily support traffic:
- fare disputes  
- lost and found  
- safety concerns  
- pickup confusion  
- routing complaints  
- ETA discrepancies  
- payment failures  
- cancellation disagreements  
- identity verification issues  
- inappropriate communication  
- app performance problems  

Without automation and forensic context, support becomes slow, expensive, and inconsistent. With automation, support becomes:
- faster  
- fairer  
- scalable  
- more accurate  
- less emotionally draining for riders & drivers  
- deeply integrated with trust/safety systems  

Barbados adds additional complexity:
- tourism-driven support volume  
- foreign phone numbers  
- roaming users with SMS-only communication  
- local cultural expectations for friendliness and explanation  
- nighttime nightlife clusters producing sudden dispute surges  

Today I designed the entire support intelligence stack.

## Activities

### 1. Built the Support Classification Engine
Every incoming issue is categorized using:
- NLP models  
- keyword extraction  
- sentiment analysis  
- metadata awareness (trip ID, time, hex zone)  

Primary categories:
1. **Fare Issues**  
2. **Trip Experience Issues**  
3. **Pickup Problems**  
4. **Safety Concerns**  
5. **Driver Behavior**  
6. **Rider Behavior**  
7. **App/Technical Issues**  
8. **Payment Problems**  
9. **Identity Verification Issues**  
10. **Lost & Found**  

The classifier also detects multi-layer issues (e.g., fare + driver behavior + routing confusion).

---

### 2. Implemented Auto-Triage with Priority Levels
The system assigns priority based on:
- issue severity  
- safety indicators  
- trip state  
- historical pattern of user behavior  
- risk scoring (for both rider & driver)  

Levels:
- **P0 — Critical (safety, harassment, threats, SOS)**  
- **P1 — High (payment failures, identity lockouts)**  
- **P2 — Medium (fare disputes, route confusion)**  
- **P3 — Low (UI complaints, minor feedback)**  

Support staff focuses where it matters most.

---

### 3. Built the Automated Resolution Engine
Many cases can be auto-resolved instantly.

Examples:
- Fare disputes → auto-recalculate using trip forensics  
- Fee waivers → under certain ETA/routing conditions  
- Cancellation refunds → based on pickup truth-data  
- Duplicate charges → immediate reversal  
- Missing receipt → regenerate automatically  
- Lost item ping → notify driver directly  

Automation dramatically reduces ticket volume.

---

### 4. Integrated Trip Forensics Into Support
Support sees:
- trip playback  
- route deviations

- braking/acceleration events

- weather and traffic context

### 5. Created the “Dispute Fairness Rulebook”
Rules consider:
- road conditions  
- weather  
- pickup-zone congestion  
- rider walking patterns  
- routing engine recommendations  
- driver navigation precision  
- device connectivity issues  

Drivers are not penalized for:
- GPS jitter  
- poor connectivity in rural areas  
- traffic slowdowns  
- unavoidable roundabout loops  

Riders are protected from:
- unfair detours  
- improper pickup handling  
- unsafe drop-offs  

Fairness strengthens loyalty across both groups.

---

### 6. Built the Lost & Found Workflow
Lost items are common, especially with tourists.

Flow:
1. Rider reports lost item  
2. Driver receives secure ping  
3. Driver confirms/denies  
4. System arranges meetup or delivery  
5. Fees processed automatically  
6. Temporary masked chat enabled  

Tourists often lose:
- sunglasses  
- wallets  
- beach gear  
- hotel keys  

Workflow must feel effortless.

## Extended Reflection (Daily Only)

Mobility is a high-contact industry — issues happen constantly. What matters is *how quickly and fairly they are resolved*.

Three insights today:

### **1. Support is trust at scale.**
A single ticket handled poorly can destroy a user’s perception of the entire platform.

### **2. Automation is only valuable if it’s empathetic.**
Users don’t want robotic responses — they want understanding backed by data.

### **3. Humans handle nuance; machines handle pattern.**
Automation solves:
- known problems  
- predictable disputes  
- obvious adjustments  

Humans solve:
- emotional cases  
- gray-area situations  
- complex safety issues  

A blended approach is the future.

Today’s work ensures that Bimride is not just technically strong, but emotionally intelligent — resolving issues fast, fairly, and with empathy.
